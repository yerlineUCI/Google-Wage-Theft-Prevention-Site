Wage Theft: Why is it important?

  • Despite being against the law, employees continue to suffer minimum wage, overtime, and meal break  
    violations amidst many other forms.

  • Underpaid minimum wage workers lose an average of $64 per week, losing about $3,300 per year.           

  • Wage theft is mostly common among young workers, women, immigrant workers, and minorities who
    have trouble standing up for themselves.

    • What are we doing about it?

    Our group, Under Construction, is in the process of creating a social to connect employees with legal options, resources,
    and other workers who have experienced similar situations. We have also created a digital calculator to help keep track of
    your daily income in order to compare your estimated total with your actual check. Our built in alerts will notify you if
    there is any significant difference that may require you to talk to your boss/managers and any legal alternatives.
